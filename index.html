🟡 Overall Risk: MODERATE — Tight but Manageable

2nd Employer (PRIMARY) is tight. Normal case gives decision ~June 1-5, only 4-8 days buffer. If LCA takes 15 days (certified May 20), premium decision lands ~June 10 — one day AFTER expiry. This is the main risk.
Leaniar (BACKUP) is strong. LCA filed Apr 21, decision ~May 21-26. Even worst-case 15-day LCA → decision ~Jun 2. 7 days buffer. Leaniar approval = insurance policy.
3rd Employer safety net unchanged. I-129 May 31-Jun 6 guarantees receipt before June 9 for portability.

📊 2nd Employer Scenarios (YOUR employer)

✅ Best case: LCA cert May 8 → I-129+Prem May 8 → Decision June 1. 8 days buffer.
🟡 Normal case: LCA cert May 13 → I-129+Prem May 13 → Decision June 3. 6 days buffer.
🟠 Slow LCA (15 days): Cert May 20 → I-129 May 20 → Decision ~June 10. 1 DAY OVER! ⚠️

🟡 Normal case: LCA cert May 5 → I-129+Prem May 5 → Decision May 26. 14 days buffer.

⚠️ Key Risk: 2nd Employer LCA Speed

The #1 risk is 2nd Employer's LCA taking 15+ days. If it certifies after May 17, premium decision may slip past June 9. Mitigation: Monitor LCA daily from May 8. If not certified by May 15, start contingency planning.
Action if 2nd Employer LCA slow: Even without 2nd Employer approval, Leaniar approval (by ~May 26) gives you valid H-1B status. You can work for 2nd Employer under portability with the receipt, while Leaniar approval keeps your status active.

🛡️ Safety Layers (3 deep)

Layer 1: Leaniar premium approval ~May 21-26. Earliest possible H-1B approval.
Layer 2: 2nd Employer premium approval ~June 1-5. Your real employer approval.
Layer 3: 3rd Employer receipt before Jun 9. Portability guaranteed.
With 3 layers, the probability of having NO status before June 9 is essentially zero.
